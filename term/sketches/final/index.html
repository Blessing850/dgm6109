Sleep Data Visualization Proposals

Name: Blessing Chizarum Amadi

For this project, I collected daily sleep data from February 4 to February 22. Each day I recorded how many hours I slept, how long I stayed awake before falling asleep, what time I went to bed (grouped into categories), and I rated my sleep quality on a scale from 1 to 5.

Sleep quality scale: 1 = very poor 2 = poor 3 = average 4 = good 5 = excellent

Visualization 1: Scatterplot with Multiple Variables

a. Hypothesis:
As the number of hours slept increases, sleep quality increases. Additionally, longer periods spent awake before falling asleep will negatively affect sleep quality.

b. Data Used:

  • Hours slept
  • Sleep quality
  • Minutes awake before sleeping
  • Bedtime category

c. Units and Range:

  • Hours slept: measured in hours, about 6–10 hours
  • Sleep quality: scale from 1–5
  • Minutes awake: measured in minutes, about 5–50 minutes
  • Bedtime category: before 11pm, 11pm–1am, after 1am

d. Representation:
X-axis = hours slept Y-axis = sleep quality Bubble size = minutes awake Color = bedtime category

Visualization 2: Side-by-Side Comparison

a. Hypothesis:
Which affects my sleep quality more: hours slept or time awake before sleeping?

b. Data Used:

  • Hours slept
  • Minutes awake before sleeping
  • Sleep quality

c. Units and Range:

  • Hours slept: 6–10 hours
  • Minutes awake: 5–50 minutes
  • Sleep quality: 1–5 scale

d. Representation:
Two scatterplots placed side by side. Left graph: hours slept vs sleep quality. Right graph: minutes awake vs sleep quality. Both graphs use the same sleep quality scale.

Visualization 3: Calendar Heatmap

a. Hypothesis:
When I go to bed later or have inconsistent bedtimes, my sleep quality is lower.

b. Data Used:

  • Date (February 4–22)
  • Sleep quality
  • Bedtime category

c. Units and Range:

  • Date: daily entries over 19 days
  • Sleep quality: 1–5 scale
  • Bedtime category: before 11pm, 11pm–1am, after 1am

d. Representation:
Each day is shown as a square in a calendar layout. Color intensity shows sleep quality (lighter = lower, darker = higher). A red dot will be added inside the square if bedtime was after 1am.
